Quick History on GNOME Engagement

GNOME Journal

  • Started in 2000
  • The idea was to create engaging content on what GNOME was doing
  • Was hard because as we are leaning, nobody really knew what goes into a release!
  • IRC channel - #gnome-journal
  • fun facts: Working on GNOME Journal - spawned the yelp help browser, and gnome-doc-utils, more than half the people working on gnome journal ended up on the Canonical community team.

Marketing

GNOME Journal went away after 2003 or 2004. The channel was renamed to #marketing.
  • We basically talked about how to engage with audiences, and show off GNOME tech.
  • It wasn't super active.
  • During the prime GNOME days when GNOME had a lot of money from consulting companies, Intel and Nokia

Engagement

Marketing was renamed to engagement after 2011 when GNOME 3 was released. The idea was that we had to deal with a lot of fall out from that release. It was crazy times. So, we wanted to make sure that we were in control of the messaging.
  • More engagement at conferences
  • Swag showing off the brand
  • Control of the GNOME irc channels on freenode.
  • Took over existing /r/gnome Reddit channel
  • Actively engaging about GNOME 3 in various social media areas.

Engagement has been successful
